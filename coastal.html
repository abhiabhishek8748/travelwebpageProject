Karnataka's coastal region, also known as the Karavali Coast, is a popular tourist destination due to its pristine beaches, rich cultural heritage, and mouth-watering cuisine. The coastal region extends from the border of Goa in the north to the border of Kerala in the south, covering a distance of about 320 kilometers. Some of the popular beaches in Karnataka's coastal region include Gokarna, Om Beach, Kudle Beach, and Murudeshwar Beach, which offer a range of water sports and adventure activities such as jet skiing, banana boat rides, parasailing, and snorkeling. Many of these beaches also have shacks and cafes that offer delicious seafood and local delicacies.

The coastal region is also known for its rich cultural heritage, with several ancient temples and historical landmarks. The Mahabaleshwar Temple in Gokarna, the Kukke Subramanya Temple in Mangalore, and the Udupi Sri Krishna Temple are some of the famous temples in the region. The historic forts of Kundapura, Bekal, and Basavaraja Durga are also popular tourist attractions. The coastal region is also famous for its traditional art and craft, such as the Yakshagana and Bhoota Kola dance forms and the Kasuti embroidery. Tourists can also indulge in the local cuisine, which includes seafood delicacies, the famous Udupi cuisine, and the unique Neer Dosa and Korri Gassi dishes.

Overall, Karnataka's coastal tourism offers a unique blend of adventure, culture, and cuisine, making it a popular destination for both domestic and international tourists.
